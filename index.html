Educate yourself Scam Safety Tips

üí≥ Mobile Money and Banking Scams
  • Never send money to someone you haven't met personally.
  • Be suspicious of urgent money requests.
  • Confirm transfers with the recipient before sending.
  • Never share PINs, passwords, or OTP codes.
  • Check the recipient's name carefully before confirming.
  • Report suspicious numbers to your mobile money provider.
üíº Recruitment and Job Scams
  • Legitimate companies do not ask for fees upfront.
  • Be wary of "work from home" offers with unrealistic earnings.
  • Verify the company via official contacts.
  • Do not provide personal documents to unverified employers.
üí∞ Online Buying and Selling Scams
  • Meet in public, safe locations when possible.
  • Avoid paying in advance for unverifiable goods.
  • Check seller reviews or history online.
  • Be cautious of deals that seem "too good to be true".
üåç Social Media and Online Accounts
  • Do not accept friend requests or messages from unknown people offering money or prizes.
  • Check profiles carefully; scammers may impersonate friends or official pages.
  • Never click on suspicious links.
  • Report suspicious accounts immediately.
  • Keep privacy settings strict.
üìß Email and Phishing Scams
  • Check sender addresses carefully.
  • Do not open attachments or click links from unknown senders.
  • Ignore emails claiming prizes, urgent payments, or account issues.
  • Use two-factor authentication on email accounts.
üí∞ Investments and Forex Scams
  • Avoid "get rich quick" schemes.
  • Never send money to strangers promising guaranteed returns.
  • Verify investment companies with regulatory authorities.
  • Keep records of all financial transactions.
üåê General Safety Tips
  • Trust your instincts if it feels wrong, it probably is.
  • Share suspected scams with friends or platforms like Scam Shield.
  • Keep personal data private.
  • Verify news before sharing to avoid scam chains.
üë• Community Awareness
  • Join local groups reporting scams.
  • Educate younger family members about online and offline scams.
  • Report repeat offenders to authorities when possible.
